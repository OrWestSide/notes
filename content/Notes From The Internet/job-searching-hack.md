### Hidden Resume Keyword Stuffing
A tactic where applicants copy the required skills and qualifications from a job description, paste them into their resume, change the font color to white (or otherwise hide the text), and submit it to increase keyword matches in Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS).

The idea is simple: many ATS tools scan resumes for exact keyword matches before a human ever sees them. By embedding the job description’s keywords invisibly, the candidate attempts to artificially boost their compatibility score and bypass automated filters.

### Reality check:
* Some ATS platforms strip formatting and will still read hidden text.
* Others flag mismatches between visible content and keyword density.
* Recruiters who discover this typically view it as deceptive.
* If the hidden content contradicts the actual experience, it can quickly disqualify the applicant.

### Bottom line
It’s an attempt to exploit automated screening rather than improve qualifications. It may work in limited cases, but it carries reputational and professional risk if detected.
